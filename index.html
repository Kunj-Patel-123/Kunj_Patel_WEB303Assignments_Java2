• About Virat Kohli
    Virat Kohli (born 5 November 1988) is a professional cricketer and former Indian international cricketer, who plays as a right-handed batsman. He captained the India men's national cricket team in limited-overs formats from 2013 to 2021 and in Test cricket from 2014 to 2021.
  • Personal Life
    Kohli married Anushka Sharma, a Bollywood actress, on 11 December 2017. The couple has a daughter named Vamika. Kohli is known for his fitness and has a chain of gyms called "Chisel."
  • Playing Style
    Kohli is a right-handed batsman and has a strong technique. He is known for his excellent cover drives and ability to chase down targets in limited-overs cricket.
  • Awards and Achievements
    2018: Rajiv Gandhi Khel Ratna, India's highest sporting honor.
    2017–18, 2018–19: Sir Garfield Sobers Trophy for ICC Cricketer of the Year.
    ICC ODI Cricketer of the Year: 2012, 2017, 2018, 2019, 2020.
    ICC Test Cricketer of the Year: 2018.
    Padma Shri: 2017, India's fourth-highest civilian award.

Kohli has been a key player for the Royal Challengers Bangalore (RCB) in the Indian Premier League (IPL). He has consistently been one of the leading run-scorers in the tournament and has a huge fan following.
Kohli holds several records in ODI cricket, including being the fastest to score 8,000, 9,000, and 10,000 runs. He is known for his remarkable consistency and aggressive batting in ODI matches.
Kohli has been a vital part of the Indian Test team, known for his resilience and leadership as the captain. He has played match-winning innings in Test matches both as a batsman and a captain.
